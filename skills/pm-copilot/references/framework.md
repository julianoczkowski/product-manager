# The Pragmatic Framework™ — canonical reference

> The proven blueprint for creating and marketing products people want to buy.
> © Pragmatic Institute. This plugin operationalizes the framework; it is not affiliated with or endorsed by Pragmatic Institute.

The framework is **market-driven** ("outside-in"): product decisions are grounded in
validated market evidence, not internal opinion. It reads left→right as
**STRATEGY → EXECUTION** across **7 columns / 37 boxes**.

- **STRATEGY (left)** — *what* to build and *why*: Market, Focus, Business, and the strategy side of Planning. Product Management leads here.
- **EXECUTION (right)** — *how* and *when* to deliver, sell and support: Programs, Enablement, Support. Product Marketing / Marketing lead here.

## The 7 columns and 37 boxes (verbatim definitions)

### MARKET — understand the market from the outside in
- **Market Problems** — Discover, collect, analyze and communicate problems found in the market. Validate these problems are urgent, pervasive and that the market is willing to pay to address them.
- **Win/Loss Analysis** — Understand why recent evaluators of the product did or did not buy and what steps they took in the buying process.
- **Distinctive Competencies** — Articulate and leverage the organization's unique abilities to deliver value to the market.
- **Competitive Landscape** — Identify competitive and alternative offerings in the market. Assess their strengths and weaknesses. Develop a strategy for winning against the competition.
- **Asset Assessment** — Inventory your assets (technical, skills, services, patents, other) and determine ways they can be leveraged.

### FOCUS — turn market knowledge into product strategy
- **Market Definition** — Map needs with target markets and analyze the market segments to actively pursue. Ensure targeted segments are large enough to support current and future business.
- **Distribution Strategy** — Determine which channels best align with your markets' buying preferences.
- **Product Portfolio** — Integrate products into a coherent portfolio focused on the market. Manage the portfolio like a "product."
- **Product Roadmap** — Illustrate the vision and key phases of deliverables for the product. *The roadmap is a plan, not a commitment.*

### BUSINESS — justify and sustain the business case
- **Business Plan** — Objective analysis of a potential market opportunity as a basis for investment. Articulate what you learned in the market and quantify the risk, including a financial model. Make a go/no-go recommendation.
- **Pricing** — Establish a pricing model, schedules, guidelines and procedures.
- **Buy, Build or Partner** — Determine the most effective way to deliver a complete solution to an identified market problem.
- **Product Profitability** — Monitor and analyze KPIs for market performance and profit contribution (use adoption/usage for internal products).
- **Innovation** — Focus the team's creative spirit on solving market problems by leveraging the organization's distinctive competencies.

### PLANNING — define who you serve and what you'll build/message
- **Positioning** — Describe the product by its ability to solve market problems. Internal positioning documents drive external messages per key buyer/persona.
- **Buyer Experience** — Research and document the buying process target personas use; understand the barriers they encounter.
- **Buyer Personas** — Define the archetypical buyers involved in purchasing your products and services.
- **User Personas** — Define the archetypical users of your products or services.
- **Requirements** — Articulate and prioritize personas and their problems so the appropriate products can be built.
- **Use Scenarios** — Illustrate market problems in a "story" that puts the problem in context. A component of requirements.
- **Stakeholder Communications** — Manage proactive communications with relevant stakeholders from strategy through execution.

### PROGRAMS — go-to-market and demand programs
- **Marketing Plan** — Strategies and tactics to generate awareness and leads for the fiscal period, with programs, events, measurements and goals.
- **Revenue Growth** — Plans and budgets for selling to new customers.
- **Revenue Retention** — Plans and budgets for loyalty and selling more to existing customers.
- **Launch** — Plan, execute and measure effectiveness of strategic launches.
- **Awareness** — Programs raising brand profile within strategic segments.
- **Nurturing** — Programs moving prospects through the funnel to customers.
- **Advocacy** — Identify and amplify customer testimonials, case studies and references.
- **Measurement** — Measure and tune programs against corporate goals.

### ENABLEMENT (a.k.a. Readiness) — equip the selling channel
- **Sales Alignment** — Align the selling process to the way the market wants to buy.
- **Content** — Develop content per buyer persona and buying step for all go-to-market channels.
- **Sales Tools** — Create tools for a specific step of the selling process.
- **Channel Training** — Train channels on how to *sell* the product, not how to use it.

### SUPPORT — ongoing information to other groups
- **Programs / Operations / Events / Channels** — Provide needed market and solution information to support internal/external programs, operations, marketing events, and channel activities.

## Reusable Pragmatic primitives (used across skills)

**The market-problem test — build only for problems that are all three:**
1. **Urgent** — when/where is the urgency coming from, and how strong is it?
2. **Pervasive** — is it big enough to build a business on? (look for the quiet 80%)
3. **Willing to pay** — painful enough that someone will pay *you* to solve it?

**The four market segments (each "a person with a problem"):**
| Segment | Lens | Question to ask |
|---|---|---|
| Customers (bought yours) | your product | "What other problems should we solve for you that we don't today?" |
| Competitors' Customers | competitor's product | "What problems should we solve that your current product doesn't?" |
| Evaluators (shopping now) | win/loss — don't disrupt the sale | "Why did we win (or lose) your business?" |
| Potentials (not shopping) | no lens — best for innovation | "What problems are you facing, and why aren't you looking for a solution?" |

**Distinctive competence test:** an attribute must be **valued + different + provable**.

**Positioning statement formula (≤25 words):**
> For **[persona]**, **[product]** **[verb]** **[problem]** by **[verb]** **[primary benefit]**.
Product description ≤50 words. Condense problems to **3–5 problem-oriented capabilities**. Positioning focuses on **problems, not features**. Write problems in the persona's **first-person** voice.

**Requirement form (Build):**
> **[persona]** has **[problem, described in a use scenario]** with **[frequency]**.

**Prioritization formulas (Build):**
- Core: **Priority = Market Evidence × Impact**
- Advanced (Four Forces): **Priority = Evidence × Impact × (B + U + C + V)** — Buying, Using, Vision, Competitive.
- **Impact scale (example, 1–5 + override):** 99999 = contractual obligation · 5 = minimum purchase criteria · 4 = potentials lose time/money · 3 = hard to achieve primary goal · 2 = difficulty with non-primary goal · 1 = not in target segment.
- **Market Evidence** = tally of documented occurrences across inputs (interviews, surveys, sales calls, support tickets, win/loss, analyst reports, reviews, usage data).

**Strategy Matrix (4×4, forced choice, ≤4 cells):**
- Columns (what to build): Improve Existing · Add New Options · Offer New Products · Invest in New Technology.
- Rows (who to win): Pursue Existing Customers · Convert Competitors' Customers · Win Potentials · Approach New Segments.

**Opportunity scoring attributes (1 = least favorable, 5 = most favorable):** Distinctive Competencies · Competitive Landscape · Investment Level · Impact to Buyers/Users.

**Roadmap vs. Release plan:** roadmap = strategic, outcome-based, time-frames not dates, *a plan not a commitment*; release plan/charter = tactical, dated, signed commitment. "Under-promise; over-deliver."

**MVP:** "the version of a new product that allows a team to collect the maximum validated learning with the least effort." MVP ≠ the smallest collection of features — it must viably solve the customer's top problems.

**Gap-analysis scale:** Importance 0–5, Assessment 0–5 (0 = not doing), **Gap = Importance − Assessment**; focus on the top 2–3 gaps; fix the strategic (left) side first.

**Stakeholder matrix (2×2):** Influence (informal authority) × Importance (formal authority) → Monitor / Keep Informed / Keep Satisfied / Manage Closely.

**Metric categories (Build):** Value · Quality · Progress · Satisfaction. "Track trends, not absolute numbers."

## Pragmatic Rules (the culture)
- Your opinion, although interesting, is irrelevant.
- An outside-in approach increases the likelihood of product success.
- The answers to most of your questions are not in the building. (**NIHITO** — Nothing Interesting Happens In The Office; ~10 market visits per PM per quarter.)
- The building is full of product experts; your company needs market experts.
- Win/loss should be done by someone not involved in that sales effort.
- Build solutions for problems that are urgent, pervasive and that the market will pay to solve.
- Positioning focuses on problems, not features. Market problems drive requirements that work.
- Remarkable products solve market problems. Executives communicate in spreadsheets, not screenshots.
- Deliver 100% of something, not 70% of everything. A roadmap is a plan, not a promise.
